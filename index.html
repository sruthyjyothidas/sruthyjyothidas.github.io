About My Research

I am an experimental nuclear physicist studying the matter under extreme conditions, recreating the first moments of the universe in laboratories on Earth, at CERN in Geneva and BNL in New York. By smashing atoms together, we create tiny droplets of Quark-Gluon Plasma (QGP). You can think of QGP as a “soup” of freely moving quarks and gluons that's trillions of degrees hot, even hotter than the center of the sun :) My research particularly focuses on pushing the limits of QGP formation (what we call as small systems) and exploring how this extreme, fluid-like matter behaves.
